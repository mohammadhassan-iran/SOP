Hello dear classmates and teachers,

My name's Mohammad Hassan Talebi, born in 1997 and raised in Tehran.


In this letter, I would like to tell you a little bit about my life story from 2014 till now.


As you know in our country Iran, after the last grade of high school, students have to participate in the university entrance exam called "KONKUR" here in this country. I passed this exam with a quite good result and my rank was 2870, which meant I had the chance of studying in a relatively good university here in Tehran. However, there was one big clear question: what did I like to study basically?


At this point of my life I was totally confused and to be honest I don't know how I came to this point that I decided to choose Computer Engineering as my field in Shahid Rajaee Teacher Training University (SRTTU). Then as you might guess, soon I realized that this field was not a good option for me and my future, but at the same time I didn't want to or wasn't brave enough to quit and start again. So this bad feeling of studying something I didn't like made me think seriously about what I could learn besides university quite well and deeply, and the answer I came up with was the English language.


Long story short, in September of 2016, I started working on my English grammar and fluency seriously, and after 2.5 years of hard work, I reached a level where I could work as a translator and teacher. After I started working, to be honest I also started feeling better and also braver, so in order to focus on my favourite job I decided to make a surprising decision in 2019 and quit SRTTU and went to PNU which is the short form of Payame Noor University. I did this because in that case I would be able to focus on my job and also my degree although it wasn't my favourite one.


Now, in this point of my life, I'm an English and Persian online tutor, and also a translator who wants to get his degree in Computer Engineering, and then obtain an MA in a field related to English in the future.


By the way, I think I should mention that the only part I like about my field a little bit is web developing, and as a result I'm familiar with HTML, CSS, JS, and PHP.


Thanks so much for reading my long story, and all the best!
